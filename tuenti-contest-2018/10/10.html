Challenge 10 - Dance

After long weeks of struggle you survived the battle against those terrible pagans! A celebration is in order!

The main event will be a group dance to honor your god and, as the senior dance instructor, you are in charge of organizing it. You're thinking about arranging everyone in a circle with the person at 12 o'clock being P0 and the next person in clockwise order being P1, etc.

Everything seemed to be going well during practice until today when you discovered a major oversight on your part: during the highlight of the dance everyone walks up to their partner and does a couple's dance. The tricky part is that partners aren't necessarily adjacent so when two partners walk towards each other they might run into other people!

To complicate matters even more, a person might hate someone and will never accept him/her as their partner.

Now, with just a few days left before the start of the grand festival you want to know how many ways you can pair everyone up so you can decide on the final arrangement.

Input

The first line has an integer C, which is the number of cases for our problem.
Each case starts with a line with two integers P and G, which are the number of people and the number of grudges. G lines follow, each with two integers A and B that indicate person PA hates person PB.

Output

For each case there should be a line starting with "Case #x: " followed by the number of different ways people can be arranged, modulo 109 + 7. Every line is followed by a new line character.

Limits

  • 1 ≤ P ≤ 1000
  • 1 ≤ G ≤ (P * (P - 1)) / 2
